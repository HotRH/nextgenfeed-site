**Trump Unveils Official White House App: ICE Reporting Feature Sparks Controversy**

The White House has launched its official mobile application for Android and iOS devices, providing users with direct access to White House content, news, and information. The app, which mirrors the existing White House website, allows users to engage with the administration’s messaging, watch live streams, and stay updated on presidential events.

In a tweet announcing the app’s availability, President Trump highlighted the feature’s ability for citizens to report individuals suspected of being in the United States illegally to U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE). The development has raised concerns among privacy advocates and immigration reform proponents, who worry about the potential misuse of this function.

The video accompanying the tweet features a tongue-in-cheek portrayal of missile launches, with an iPhone prominently displayed, rather than the rumored “Trump Phone.” While the app’s primary purpose is to disseminate information and facilitate engagement between the administration and the public, the ICE reporting feature has generated controversy, sparking debates about immigration policy, data privacy, and government surveillance.
