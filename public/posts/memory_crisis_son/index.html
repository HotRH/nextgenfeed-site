**Memory Crisis: Sony Halts Global Memory Card Sales Amid Supply Shortages**

Sony has taken the drastic measure of temporarily suspending sales of its memory card products due to supply shortages. As of March 27th, 2026, the company will no longer accept orders for nearly all CFexpress and SD memory cards, including popular models such as CFexpress Type A, Type B, and high-capacity SDXC/SDHC cards.

While a few lower-end Type B models and select SF-UZ series products will continue to be available, customers will no longer be able to purchase the majority of Sony’s memory card offerings. This sudden halt in sales is likely a response to ongoing supply chain issues affecting the global memory card market.

The move is expected to have significant implications for industries that rely heavily on Sony’s memory cards, such as professional photography and videography, where high-performance storage solutions are crucial. With alternative suppliers already feeling the strain of production constraints, customers may be forced to explore other options or wait for supply chain issues to resolve before resuming their usual workflows.

As the global demand for memory card products continues to surge, this development serves as a stark reminder of the ongoing challenges facing the industry. Sony’s decision to halt sales will likely prompt a re-evaluation of its production and distribution strategies in an effort to mitigate the impact of these supply shortages on its business and customers.
