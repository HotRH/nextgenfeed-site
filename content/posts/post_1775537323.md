The wait is finally over for Google Pixel enthusiasts in Japan, as the latest flagship device, Pixel 10a, has launched in the region with an exclusive "Isai Blue" color. This unique shade marks the fifth entry in the Pixel 10a's color palette, joining Lavender, Fog, Berry, and Obsidian. The new color is not only a treat for fans but also reflects Google's commitment to creativity and inclusivity.

The collaboration between Google and HERALBONY, a creative company that empowers artists with disabilities, has resulted in the stunning "Isai Blue" hue. This special edition Pixel 10a comes bundled with an exclusive bumper case, original stickers, and a unique wallpaper created by a Heralbony-contracted artist. Additionally, users will receive an original theme pack that harmonizes the system's icon colors to match the device's new appearance.

The "Isai Blue" variant is available for pre-order now and ships on May 20. This special edition Pixel 10a comes with 256GB of storage, making it a must-have for those who want to store more photos, videos, and files. The existing color options, Lavender, Fog, Berry, and Obsidian, will also be available in Japan.

The launch of the Pixel 10a in Japan marks an important milestone in Google's efforts to strengthen its presence in the region. With a starting price of ¥79,900, the device is poised to make a significant impact on the local market. Furthermore, Google's decision to offer boosted trade-ins on the Google Store through April 27 highlights the company's commitment to providing value to its customers.

In conclusion, the "Isai Blue" Pixel 10a is more than just a new color variant – it represents a celebration of creativity, inclusivity, and gratitude. The device's unique features, bundled with its stunning appearance, make it an attractive option for those looking to upgrade their smartphone experience in Japan.
