As the foldable smartphone market continues to evolve, a new report by Counterpoint Research suggests that Apple's entry into this space will have a profound impact on the existing players. According to the report, Google Pixel, which currently holds a 5% share of the North American foldable market, is expected to see its market share decline to just 3% in 2026. Meanwhile, Apple is poised to take nearly half (46%) of the overall foldable market in North America by the end of next year.

The report highlights Samsung's expected drop from 51% to 29%, and Motorola's decline from 44% to 23%. This significant shift in market share can be attributed to Apple's highly anticipated foldable iPhone, which is set to launch in September. The US, where Apple has a stronghold on the smartphone market, will likely see a significant influx of customers switching to the Cupertino giant's foldable offering.

The data suggests that Google Pixel's decline is largely due to its single-foldable lineup being closely aligned with Apple's expected device in terms of form factor and price positioning. This could lead to some existing demand for Google's products shifting towards Apple's ecosystem. Furthermore, Apple's entry will likely drive foldable smartphone market expansion by unlocking upgrade demand within its existing iPhone user base, particularly in the ultra-premium segment.

The report also notes that part of this demand is expected to come from replacement purchases among existing Android foldable users, implying that Apple's volumes may not be entirely incremental to the market. This could have significant implications for the Android ecosystem as a whole, particularly Samsung and Google Pixel, which will need to adapt their strategies to compete with Apple's formidable presence in the market.

As the foldable smartphone market continues to evolve, it will be interesting to see how these dynamics play out. With Apple set to launch its first foldable iPhone in September, and other players like Motorola and Samsung already gearing up for new releases, the stage is set for an exciting and potentially tumultuous year in the world of foldables.
