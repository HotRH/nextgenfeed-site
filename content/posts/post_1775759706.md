In a move that marks a significant shift in X's messaging strategy, the social network has brought back its Voice Notes feature within X Chat, its direct messaging system. This development comes as a response to user backlash 

The reintroduction of Voice Notes is particularly noteworthy in light of X's recent spinout of X Chat as a standalone app. This move reflects the company's strategy change, which aims to create distinct experiences for various features within its ecosystem. The beta test of an X Chat app on iOS, which offers access to X's upgraded DM feature, has also generated concerns about the service's security. Security experts have warned that X Chat is less secure than other encrypted messaging apps like Signal.

The return of Voice Notes marks a significant milestone in X's roadmap for its chat platform. When the company first introduced its new chat platform in November, it promised that audio messages would "return soon." With this feature now available, users can engage in private conversations using voice notes, making X Chat more appealing to those who value the flexibility of audio messaging.

The reintroduction of Voice Notes also underscores X's commitment to enhancing its chat experience. The service already offers a range of features, including the ability to edit and delete messages, block or get notified of screenshots, share files, make voice and video calls, and set messages to automatically disappear. The addition of Voice Notes is likely to further enhance user engagement and retention within X Chat.

As part of its broader strategy to create an "everything app" or a super app, X is testing separate apps for various features, such as X Money, its payments service. This move reflects the company's ambition to offer a seamless and integrated experience across multiple platforms. With the revival of Voice Notes, X is taking a significant step towards achieving this goal and making its chat platform more competitive in the market.
