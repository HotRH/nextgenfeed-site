As the tech industry grapples with the explosive impact of artificial intelligence (AI) on software development, one company is poised to capitalize on this trend: Vercel, a 10-year-old developer tool and website hosting platform. With its annual recurring revenue (ARR) soaring from $100 million in early 2024 to a run rate of $340 million by February 2026, the company's growth trajectory has sparked IPO speculation.

At the HumanX conference in San Francisco, Vercel CEO Guillermo Rauch hinted at the company's public market readiness, stating that "Vercel is very much a working public company." When asked about his plans for an initial public offering (IPO), Rauch replied that while there's no specific timeline, the company is getting more ready every day. This cautious optimism is in stark contrast to the prevailing sentiment among tech CEOs, who have gone quiet on their IPO plans amidst market volatility and AI-driven uncertainty.

Vercel's success can be attributed to its ability to capitalize on the proliferation of AI-generated apps and agents. As Rauch noted, "When I started this company, only tens of millions of people could deploy [apps]. Now we're seeing that everybody in the world can create an app." This democratization of software development has resulted in a significant surge in app creation by non-developers, with 30% of Vercel's platform already coming from AI agents. As these agents continue to accelerate software production, Vercel is well-positioned to become the primary platform for hosting their creations.

Rauch's comments also suggest that Vercel is preparing for a future where infrastructure-as-a-service (IaaS) becomes even more critical. He emphasized that "the total addressable market of infrastructure has now grown, and it simply has no ceiling." This sentiment echoes the company's $9.3 billion valuation following its Series F funding round in September, led by Accel.

As Vercel eyes a potential IPO, it will be interesting to see how the company navigates the current market landscape. With many software companies freezing their IPO plans due to concerns over AI disruption, Vercel's decision to proceed could signal a shift in sentiment. Whether or not the company goes public, its growth trajectory and strategic positioning make it an exciting player to watch in the rapidly evolving tech landscape.

The Bottom Line:

Vercel's impressive growth, fueled by AI-generated apps and agents, has propelled the company towards IPO readiness. As the market continues to evolve, Vercel's ability to capitalize on this trend could pave the way for a successful public listing. With its infrastructure-as-a-service offering and vibe coding tool v0, the company is well-positioned to become the go-to platform for hosting AI-generated software.
