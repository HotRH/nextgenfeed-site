The AI landscape has taken a significant leap forward with the debut of Anthropic's new frontier model, Mythos, which promises to revolutionize cybersecurity work. As part of its Project Glasswing initiative, 12 partner organizations will deploy Mythos to scan software systems for code vulnerabilities and secure critical software.

Mythos, touted as one of Anthropic's "most powerful" models yet, has already identified thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities, many of which are decades old. This achievement is all the more impressive considering Mythos was not specifically trained for cybersecurity work. Instead, it will be used to scan both first-party and open-source software systems for potential weaknesses.

At the heart of Mythos' capabilities lies its strong agentic coding and reasoning skills, making it a general-purpose model designed for complex tasks such as agent-building and coding. This level of sophistication is characteristic of Anthropic's frontier models, which are considered its most advanced and high-performance models.

The 12 partner organizations previewing Mythos include industry giants like Amazon, Apple, Cisco, CrowdStrike, the Linux Foundation, Microsoft, and Palo Alto Networks. These partners will ultimately share their experiences using Mythos, allowing the broader tech community to benefit from its capabilities.

While the Mythos preview is not publicly available, 40 organizations will gain access to it aside from the partnership. This controlled rollout ensures that the model's full potential can be leveraged by those who need it most – without compromising security or integrity.

Complicated Context: Government Involvement and Ongoing Discussions

Anthropic has engaged in ongoing discussions with federal officials about Mythos, although these talks are likely complicated by the company's current legal battle with the Trump administration. The Pentagon had previously labeled Anthropic a supply-chain risk due to its refusal to allow autonomous targeting or surveillance of U.S. citizens.

The Leaked Memo: A Glimpse into Mythos' Capabilities

News of Mythos was originally leaked in a data security incident reported last month by Fortune. A draft blog about the model, then called "Capybara," was left in an unsecured cache of documents available on a publicly inspectable data lake. The leak revealed that Mythos is a new tier of model, larger and more intelligent than Anthropic's Opus models.

The leaked document described Mythos as "by far the most powerful AI model we've ever developed." This claim was later corroborated by Anthropic, which emphasized the model's potential to pose a cybersecurity threat if weaponized by bad actors. However, it is reassuring that Mythos will be deployed for defensive security work rather than exploiting vulnerabilities.

Accidental Exposure: A Cautionary Tale

In a separate incident, Anthropic accidentally exposed nearly 2,000 source code files and over half a million lines of code via a mistake in the launch of version 2.1.88 of its Claude Code software package. The company then inadvertently caused thousands of code repositories on GitHub to be taken down as it attempted to clean up the mess.

In light of these events, the controlled rollout of Mythos is a testament to Anthropic's commitment to ensuring the security and integrity of its technology. As the AI landscape continues to evolve, it is essential that organizations like Anthropic prioritize transparency and accountability to maintain public trust.
