As the sun set on Monday afternoon, the crew of Artemis II made history by surpassing a record set 56 years ago by the Apollo 13 mission. With a distance of over 248,655 miles from Earth, they have now traveled farther than any humans before them. But this remarkable achievement was not just about breaking a barrier; it was also an opportunity for the crew to reflect on their journey and pay tribute to those who have inspired them along the way.

In a poignant ceremony held in lunar orbit, the crew proposed names for two craters on the Moon's surface. The first, naturally enough, was named after their spacecraft, Integrity. But the second name carried far more significance. Mission specialist Jeremy Hansen revealed that they had chosen to honor the late Carroll Wiseman, wife of commander Reid Wiseman and mother of Katie and Ellie. Wiseman passed away in 2020 at the age of 46 due to cancer.

The crater in question is a "bright spot on the Moon," a fitting tribute to the vibrant spirit of Carroll Wiseman. The crew's decision to name it after her was a heartfelt gesture, reflecting their deep sense of connection and community. As Hansen concluded the announcement, the crew gathered around each other in Integrity, sharing a tender hug that spoke volumes about the bonds they had formed during this historic mission.

The International Astronomical Union (IAU) is responsible for approving planetary surface feature names, including those on the Moon. While the IAU's formal approval process remains ahead, the Artemis II crew has already made their intentions clear. By naming these craters after Integrity and Carroll Wiseman, they have etched a lasting legacy that transcends mere geographical markers. Instead, it serves as a testament to the human spirit's capacity for perseverance, compassion, and remembrance.

As the Artemis II mission continues to push the boundaries of space exploration, its crew will undoubtedly face many more challenges and triumphs. Yet, in this moment, they have reminded us that even amidst the vast expanse of space, we are all connected by our shared experiences, emotions, and stories. The naming of these craters serves as a poignant reminder of the power of human connection to transcend the limitations of time and distance.
