Introduction

Aventon, a prominent electric bike brand, has just released its latest offering, the Current ADV, a full-suspension electric mountain bike that promises to revolutionize the way we experience trail riding. With its sleek design, excellent components, and impressive range, this e-MTB is poised to take the industry by storm. In this in-depth review, we'll delve into the features, pros, and cons of the Aventon Current ADV, exploring what makes it a must-have for any serious mountain biker.

Design and Performance

The Current ADV's design is its most striking feature. With its 6061 aluminum frame, SRAM Eagle groupset, tubeless-ready Maxxis Minion tires, and Rockshox Psylo Gold front suspension, this e-MTB looks and feels like a traditional analog mountain bike. The super-long wheelbase (on the XL-size model) adds to its stability and mass, making it feel like it's on rails when descending technical trails.

The Aventon Ultro X mid-drive motor produces an impressive 850 watts of power and 120 nm of torque, making quick work of even the steepest inclines. The bike's heft is only noticeable when loading it onto a car rack, but this only highlights its impressive carrying capacity.

Components and Range

The Current ADV boasts excellent components for the price, including a SRAM Eagle groupset, tubeless-ready Maxxis Minion tires, and Rockshox Psylo Gold front suspension. The bike's 800-watt-hour battery is tucked neatly into the downtube, providing an estimated range of up to 105 miles on a single charge.

In practice, I found that switching between power modes (Auto, Eco, Trail, Turbo, and Boost) allowed me to conserve energy while still enjoying the thrill of electric assistance. The longest day I spent in the saddle was three hours, during which the battery dropped only about 20 percent.

Limitations

While the Current ADV is an exceptional e-MTB, there are a few limitations worth noting. Firstly, the top-tube-mounted display can be distracting, especially when descending technical trails. The bike's beeps are all the same tone, making it difficult to quickly identify which power mode I'm in without taking my eyes off the trail.

Additionally, the lack of a handlebar-mounted display makes it necessary to take my eyes completely off the trail to check the computer, increasing the risk of an unintentional tumble. It would be a simple fix to differentiate between the beeps or move the display to the handlebars.

Conclusion

The Aventon Current ADV is an excellent electric mountain bike that offers impressive performance, range, and value for its price. While it's not without its limitations, the Current ADV is a game-changer for trail enthusiasts who crave the thrill of electric assistance without sacrificing the feel of traditional analog mountain biking. With its masterful design, excellent components, and impressive range, this e-MTB is sure to revolutionize the way we experience the trails.
