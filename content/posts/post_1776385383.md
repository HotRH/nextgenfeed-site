The world was blown away by Metro Exodus' impressive ray tracing capabilities in 2019, but little did we know that a global crisis would not only reshape the game industry but also have a profound impact on the development of its sequel, Metro 2039. Seven years have passed since then, and while much has changed, one constant remains: the devastating war between Ukraine and Russia.

Founded in Kyiv in 2006, 4A Studios, the developer behind Metro 2039, may be officially based in Malta, but their roots run deep in Ukrainian soil. The majority of the team working on the game is comprised of Ukrainians, who have been forced to navigate the harsh realities of war while trying to create a post-apocalyptic masterpiece. For those affected developers, it's not just about writing code or designing levels; it's about finding ways to work amidst chaos.

When asked about how the war has impacted their work, 4A Studios' representatives revealed that they've had to rely on generators and batteries for electricity and take shelter from drone attacks during the day. It's a far cry from the comfortable offices and studios most game developers are accustomed to. Despite these disruptions, the team remains committed to delivering what they believe is their best work yet. The support of their families has been instrumental in keeping morale high, allowing them to focus on the task at hand.

Growing up during Ukraine's tumultuous '90s has instilled a sense of resilience and determination within 4A Studios' developers. They've learned that ideas and achievements must be fought for, and this philosophy is reflected in their work. Metro 2039 promises to deliver a uniquely Ukrainian perspective on the war-torn world it portrays. Gone are the days of preventing conflict; instead, the game will explore the consequences of war and the choices that come with it.

As executive producer Jon Bloch notes, "The worst of humanity will be on display." With Metro 2039, players can expect a more somber and mature take on the series' established universe. The game's narrative is now deeply intertwined with the harsh realities of war, lending an air of authenticity to its themes. In this new era of gaming, where war and conflict are increasingly relevant topics, Metro 2039 serves as a powerful reminder that the consequences of our actions are far-reaching and devastating.
