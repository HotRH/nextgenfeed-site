In an era where premium features are increasingly becoming the norm, Hisense has taken a bold step with its latest midrange offering, the U7SG TV. This innovative device boasts a sleek industrial stand and anti-reflective matte screen, reminiscent of high-end displays from Samsung. Furthermore, its mini-LED backlighting is impressively bright, making it an excellent choice for gamers and movie enthusiasts alike.

However, Hisense's usual step-up model, the U8, has now become the UR8, featuring cutting-edge RGB LED display technology. This significant upgrade offers more vibrant colors, higher brightness, and improved performance. While this new tech promises exciting developments, the U7SG can't help but feel a sense of FOMO (fear of missing out) as it remains stuck in midrange limbo.

Despite its premium appearance, the U7SG has some familiar budget flaws that may disappoint enthusiasts. Specifically, side-view performance is subpar, and software issues can cause challenging scenes to appear distorted in default settings. Fortunately, these problems are easily fixable, and the TV's exceptional colors and improved image clarity make up for these minor drawbacks.

Streaming and Setup: A Solid Foundation

The U7SG's sturdy new stand is a major improvement over previous models. Its rugged design adds significant weight (10 pounds) to the 65-inch device I reviewed, but the six-screw assembly ensures a secure hold. The matte-like anti-reflective screen on the front and dual woofers at the back provide excellent sound quality. While some may still prefer external speakers or a soundbar, the U7SG's built-in audio is a significant step up from previous Hisense offerings.

In conclusion, the Hisense U7SG TV strikes an impressive balance between premium features and budget-friendly pricing. While it may not offer the cutting-edge RGB LED display technology of its higher-end counterparts, it more than makes up for this with its snappy Google TV interface, excellent mini-LED backlighting, and solid overall performance. As prices are expected to drop in the near future, the U7SG is an excellent midrange option for those seeking a reliable and feature-rich television experience.
