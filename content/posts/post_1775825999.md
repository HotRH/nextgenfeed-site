As artificial intelligence (AI) continues to permeate various aspects of life, a new report by Gallup has shed light on the sentiments of the digital-native generation, Gen Z, towards this technology. The findings suggest that enthusiasm for AI is waning, while resentment and anxiety are on the rise.

The survey, conducted in February and March 2023, polled nearly 1,600 individuals between the ages of 14 and 29 across the United States. The results indicate a significant decline in Gen Z's feelings towards AI compared to previous years. While only 18% of respondents expressed hope about AI this year, down from 27% last year, the percentage of those feeling angry or frustrated has increased to 31%, up from 22% in the past.

Furthermore, anxiety levels regarding AI have remained relatively steady at around 40%. This growing discontent may be attributed to the perceived risks associated with using AI in the workplace. According to the report, nearly half of Gen Z workers believe that the drawbacks of AI outweigh its benefits, a significant increase from last year's figures. Additionally, eight out of ten respondents agreed that relying on AI for faster work completion will make learning harder in the future.

Despite these concerns, Gen Z is not ready to abandon AI entirely. The report highlights that just over half of respondents use AI at least weekly, with 56% acknowledging its potential to help them complete tasks more efficiently. Moreover, many believe they will need AI skills for higher education and their future careers. This ambivalence can be attributed to the recognition of AI's utility in certain contexts while also acknowledging its long-term impact on learning, trust, and career readiness.

As AI settles into a more mature technology, its consequences are becoming increasingly apparent. The report's findings suggest that Gen Z is grappling with the reality of AI's presence in their lives, education, and careers. This generation, already navigating the challenges of mass layoffs and adapting to the rapid proliferation of AI, must also contend with growing distrust between the general public and companies racing to develop this technology.

In conclusion, the Gallup report presents a nuanced picture of Gen Z's relationship with AI. While enthusiasm has waned, resentment and anxiety have increased, reflecting concerns about AI's impact on learning, trust, and career readiness. As this technology continues to evolve, it is essential to recognize the complexities of Gen Z's attitudes towards AI and their desire for a balanced approach that leverages its benefits while addressing its limitations.
