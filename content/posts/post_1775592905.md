The wait is almost over for Rivian's highly anticipated R2, an electric SUV that promises to disrupt the midsize market segment with its impressive range and performance capabilities. As we approach the launch date, the automaker has quietly revealed some exciting details about this new model, which will initially be available in two trim levels: Performance and Premium.

At first glance, the R2's range may not seem like a significant departure from other electric vehicles on the market. However, upon closer inspection, it becomes clear that Rivian has made significant strides in optimizing its battery technology to deliver an impressive 335 miles of range on a single charge, as certified by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA). This achievement is all the more remarkable when considering that the R2's Performance trim comes equipped with 21-inch tires, which typically would result in reduced range due to increased drag.

But what about the smaller wheels, you might ask? Typically, switching from larger to smaller tires would increase an electric vehicle's range. Not so in this case. According to EPA estimates, the R2 with 20-inch wheels and all-terrain tires manages a respectable 314 miles of range. This slight decrease is likely due to the knobby design of these tires, which generate more drag than smoother tires.

As Rivian continues to roll out its trim levels, we can expect further insights into the vehicle's performance capabilities. With the Premium trim set to launch in late 2026 and a lower-cost option with a smaller battery package arriving in late 2027, there will be plenty of opportunities for the company to showcase its technological advancements.

Beyond the impressive range figures, the R2 document reveals some additional details that are sure to delight electric vehicle enthusiasts. Notably, the pack capacity is listed as 86.8 kWh, slightly lower than the official Rivian specs, and maximum DC fast-charging rates have been boosted to an impressive 210 kW. Additionally, the heat pump has been upgraded from its predecessor in the R1, providing improved performance and efficiency.

As Rivian continues to push the boundaries of electric vehicle technology, it's clear that the R2 is set to make a significant impact on the market. With its impressive range, capable performance, and commitment to innovation, this midsize SUV is poised to captivate audiences and redefine what we expect from an electric vehicle.
