In a landmark announcement, Deezer has revealed that 44% of new music uploaded to its platform daily is AI-generated. This staggering figure represents a significant surge in AI-generated music uploads, with almost 75,000 tracks per day and over two million per month flooding the platform. The proliferation of AI-generated content has far-reaching implications for the music industry, raising questions about authenticity, artist rights, and fan transparency.

The numbers are striking: since June 2025, Deezer has tagged more than 13.4 million AI tracks on its platform. This trend is unlikely to slow down anytime soon, with AI-generated music consumption accounting for a mere 1-3% of total streams on the platform. However, this low percentage belies the sheer volume of AI content being uploaded daily, highlighting the need for platforms like Deezer to implement robust detection tools and transparency measures.

Deezer's AI-music detection tool has been instrumental in identifying and removing AI-generated tracks from algorithmic recommendations and editorial playlists. The company has also taken steps to demonetize 85% of fraudulent streams detected as AI-generated, ensuring that artists receive fair compensation for their work. This proactive approach sets a precedent for the industry, emphasizing the importance of safeguarding artist rights and promoting transparency.

The latest survey conducted by Deezer further underscores the need for greater transparency in AI-generated music. A whopping 97% of respondents couldn't distinguish between fully AI-generated tracks and human-made music, highlighting the ease with which AI-generated content can blend in seamlessly with authentic works. The survey also revealed that 52% of respondents believe 100% AI-generated songs should not be included in main charts alongside human-made songs, while 80% agree that AI-generated music should be clearly labeled for listeners.

As AI-generated music continues to gain traction, the industry is faced with a critical choice: adapt and innovate or risk being left behind. The rise of AI-generated music is no longer a marginal phenomenon; it's an integral part of the music ecosystem. It's time for platforms like Deezer, Spotify, Apple Music, and others to come together and establish standardized measures for detecting, labeling, and promoting AI-generated content.

In this new landscape, the stakes are high. Artists must be protected from the dilution of their rights, while fans deserve clarity about what they're listening to. The music industry's future depends on its ability to navigate these challenges and harness the power of AI-generated music for the benefit of all stakeholders.
