In a move that sends shockwaves through Microsoft's developer community, Julia Liuson, the long-serving head of the company's Developer Division (DevDiv), has announced her resignation after 34 years at the helm. As the leader of DevDiv for the past 12 years, Liuson played a crucial role in shaping Microsoft's developer-centric strategy, particularly during the period when the company focused on open source initiatives and acquired GitHub for $7.5 billion.

Liuson's departure is significant not only because of her impressive tenure but also due to the timing. The news comes less than a year after Thomas Dohmke, former CEO of GitHub, resigned from his post, leaving a power vacuum at the helm of Microsoft's prized asset. While Liuson was responsible for overseeing GitHub revenue, engineering, and support, her departure raises questions about who will fill the void and whether the DevDiv team will report directly to CoreAI chief Jay Parikh in the coming months.

Liuson's memo announcing her resignation highlights her pride in DevDiv being recognized as one of the most customer-obsessed teams, delivering product truth where customers choose to use Microsoft products. Her departure marks a symbolic shift in Microsoft's strategy, particularly in its developer-focused initiatives. With Liuson's exit, the company may be poised to re-evaluate its priorities and potentially pivot towards new opportunities.
