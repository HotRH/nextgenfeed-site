In a move that signals Google's commitment to refining its Pixel-exclusive features, the tech giant has released an update to its newly launched Now Playing app for Pixel phones. The update, which rolled out gradually over the past few days, brings with it version 2026.03.24.x, a minor bump from the previous release (2026.03.02.x).

At first glance, the update appears to be focused on squashing bugs and ironing out any wrinkles in the user experience. Indeed, we've noticed no outward changes to the app's functionality or design after installing the new version. This approach suggests that Google is prioritizing stability over flashy new features at this stage. The "What's new" release notes in the Play Store listing remain unchanged, further supporting the notion that this update is a behind-the-scenes fixer.

However, some users have reported seeing a subtle tweak to the Now Playing icon on their lockscreen. Specifically, they've noticed a message reading "Tap to see what's playing" appearing beneath the fingerprint icon. This prompt bears some resemblance to the previous version's behavior, which would trigger an online search if your Pixel phone recognized a song was playing but couldn't identify it using its onboard database.

The new redesign offers a lockscreen shortcut and Quick Settings Tile as alternatives to this notification. These changes could be seen as a nod towards greater interactivity with the Now Playing feature, potentially paving the way for future enhancements. One possible interpretation of the "Tap to see what's playing" prompt is that it serves as an invitation to users to favorite songs, view cover art, and open their music app – all from within the lockscreen.

While this update may not seem like a major milestone in itself, it could be indicative of Google's plans for future developments. Typically, first-party apps receive significant updates alongside Pixel Drops every three months. As such, we can't help but wonder if this is merely a minor patch or a sign that more substantial upgrades are on the horizon. Only time will tell whether Now Playing will evolve to become an integral part of the Pixel experience or remain a niche feature.
