As the world of technology continues to evolve, it's essential to stay informed about the latest developments in the market. In this article, we'll delve into the best iPads to buy and those that you should avoid in 2026.

Understanding the Current iPad Lineup

Apple currently offers four main iPad models: Air, Pro, Mini, and base iPad. Each device has its unique strengths, making it crucial to know what you're buying and what you should pay for it. This comprehensive guide covers the iPad models available, their key differences, and the older versions that are still in use.

When is a Good Time to Buy an iPad?

The answer is now! The latest M4 iPad Air was updated in March 2026, while the base iPad and iPad Pro were refreshed last year. The majority of users should opt for one of these models, which are great options. There were rumors that the base iPad would receive an update this spring, but it didn't materialize. Therefore, the next likely launch time is this fall.

iPadOS 26: A Game-Changer for Multitasking

In September 2025, Apple released iPadOS 26, which brought a whole new multitasking experience to the table. This update allows users to resize apps and have multiple floating windows, making it easier to work with multiple tasks simultaneously.

Which iPads are Compatible with iPadOS 26?

The following iPads are compatible with iPadOS 26:

* iPad (8th gen and later)
* iPad Mini (5th gen and later)
* iPad Air (3rd gen and later)
* iPad Pro 11-inch (1st gen and later)
* iPad Pro 12.9-inch (3rd gen and later)
* iPad Pro (M4 and M5)

Tips for Finding Your iPad Model

If you're unsure about your iPad model, follow these simple steps:

1. Look at the back of your iPad; the model number starting with the letter A is etched on the back cover.
2. Visit Apple's website and search for your corresponding model number.

Avoiding Older iPads

While it may be tempting to opt for an older iPad, there are some models that you should avoid:

* iPad Mini 1 through 6: These devices have low-resolution, pixelated screens or are just too old.
* iPad 1 through 10: These devices also have low-resolution screens or are outdated.

In conclusion, choosing the right iPad can be a daunting task. By understanding the current lineup, knowing when to buy, and avoiding older models, you'll be well on your way to finding the perfect device for your needs.
