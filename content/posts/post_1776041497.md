The moon has long been a source of fascination, inspiring countless works of art, music, and literature. In recent years, it seems that the lunar theme has gained particular significance, with several notable releases exploring its mysteries. As I delved into my own musical recommendations for the week, Kate NV's 2020 album "Room for the Moon" emerged as a natural fit. This experimental pop masterpiece is a true gem, defying conventions and embracing the whimsical in equal measure.

At first glance, "Room for the Moon" may appear to be a collection of disparate sounds, drawn from Russian and Japanese pop from the 1970s and 1980s, as well as children's movies. However, upon closer inspection, it becomes clear that these influences have been reimagined through Kate Shilonosova's unique lens. The result is a record that is both nostalgic and futuristic, blending Talking Heads-esque basslines with proggy synths and reverbed drum machines.

The album opener "Not Not Not" sets the tone for the rest of the release, with its chaotic melodies dancing around each other in a perpetually disorienting way. This sense of uncertainty is a hallmark of Kate NV's songwriting, as she expertly navigates the boundaries between order and chaos. The instrumental "Da Na" follows, showcasing her ability to craft mesmerizing soundscapes that are both familiar and uncanny.

Perhaps the most striking aspect of "Room for the Moon" is its ability to balance the fantastical with a sense of hope. Tracks like "Sayonara (Full Moon Version)" and "Plans" evoke the dreamlike quality of Oingo Boingo's new wave theatrics, while also conveying a sense of optimism. Even the album's more unsettling moments, such as the saxophone solo on "Plans," are tempered by Kate NV's ability to find beauty in the strange.

Despite the album's experimental nature, there is an undeniable lightness and innocence at its core. The lyrics, mostly written in Russian, may be lost on some listeners, but the sense of hope and wonder they convey is palpable. "Room for the Moon" is not a somber lunar lullaby, but rather a delightful daydream that invites listeners to join Kate NV on her fantastical journey.

Available on Bandcamp and major streaming services including Qobuz, Deezer, Apple Music, YouTube Music, and Spotify, "Room for the Moon" is an essential release for fans of experimental pop. Its unique blend of nostalgia and futurism, combined with its sense of hope and wonder, make it a true standout in the world of contemporary music.
