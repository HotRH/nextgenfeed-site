In a shocking turn of events, a father found himself entangled in a frustrating support nightmare after his teenage daughter's Discord account was hacked. The ordeal highlights the shortcomings of Discord's support infrastructure and raises questions about the platform's preparedness to handle situations involving minor victims.

The story began when Brady Frey's 12-year-old daughter created a Discord account, falsifying her age to bypass the platform's minimum age requirement. This is not an uncommon occurrence, as many young users are eager to join online communities, even if it means circumventing safety measures designed to protect them. The incident underscores the need for platforms like Discord to implement robust age verification processes.

When Frey's daughter was hacked and her account taken over by an attacker posing as Discord support, he assumed that the platform would spring into action to address the issue. However, his expectations were dashed when the chatbot, Clyde, and a supposed human support member, Nelly, closed her support tickets without providing any meaningful assistance. This lack of understanding and empathy from Discord's support team is staggering, particularly in light of the potential harm that could have been inflicted on dozens of young friends who were also being targeted by the same social engineering tactics.

Frey's experience highlights the need for platforms like Discord to develop a more robust support infrastructure that acknowledges the unique challenges faced by minor users. In this case, the father was forced to navigate a seemingly impenetrable bureaucracy, only to be met with automated responses and dismissive attitude from the support team. The fact that it took intervention from Ars Technica for the issue to be resolved is a stark reminder of the need for platforms like Discord to prioritize the safety and well-being of their young users.

The incident also raises questions about Discord's willingness to update its age setting policies to better protect minors. In an era where online safety is increasingly becoming a top concern, it is essential that platforms take proactive measures to ensure that young users are not exploited or harmed by malicious actors. The experience of Brady Frey and his daughter serves as a stark reminder of the need for platforms like Discord to prioritize the well-being of their young users and develop support infrastructure that acknowledges the unique challenges faced by minor victims.
