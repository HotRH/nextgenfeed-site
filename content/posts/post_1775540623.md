The latest update from Google Photos is set to revolutionize the way users edit their photos and videos, introducing two groundbreaking features that are rolling out now for Android users worldwide. The first feature, "AI Enhance," is a game-changer in photo editing, allowing users to apply a magic fix to their images using artificial intelligence (AI). This do-it-all button skips over extensive editing and focuses on adjusting lighting and contrast levels with a single touch.

The AI Enhance tool is designed to provide a quick and effortless way for users to enhance their photos. By leveraging the power of machine learning, Google Photos can automatically detect and adjust various aspects of an image, such as brightness, saturation, and exposure, to produce a more visually appealing result. This feature is particularly useful for those who want to preserve the original quality of their photos but still want to make some adjustments without investing hours in editing.

The second major update is video playback speed controls, which are also rolling out now for Android users. This highly requested feature allows users to adjust the playback speed of their videos from 0.25x to 2x using the three-dot menu. This means that users can now fast-forward or rewind through long videos with ease, making it a great addition for those who want to quickly review or share video content.

The introduction of video playback speed controls is particularly significant because it addresses a common pain point for users who often find themselves stuck in slow-motion or forced to watch long videos at the same pace. With this new feature, users can now control the pace of their video viewing experience, making it more engaging and convenient.

While the AI Enhance button appears to be rolling out globally, the video playback speed controls may not be available worldwide just yet, suggesting that this feature is still in its early stages. Nevertheless, both features are expected to begin appearing on Android devices shortly, bringing a new level of sophistication and flexibility to Google Photos.

In related news, Google Photos has made some significant changes to its editing shortcuts, moving some features to the "reduce accidental triggers" category. Additionally, the company has announced the end of unlimited storage for subscribers, marking the final remaining subscription option.
