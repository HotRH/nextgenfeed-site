In a move that signals a significant shift in the luxury automotive landscape, Mercedes-Benz has unveiled its first all-electric C-Class, boasting an impressive range of up to 762 kilometers (473 miles) on the WLTP cycle. Dubbed the C 400 4MATIC, this latest iteration of the iconic compact sedan redefines the benchmark for electric vehicles in the premium segment.

Uncompromising Performance and Efficiency

The C 400 4MATIC's impressive performance credentials are rooted in its 800-volt architecture, designed to optimize efficiency and long-distance travel. With an estimated range of up to 762 kilometers (473 miles), this EV is poised to rival its gasoline-powered counterparts in terms of practicality. The car's 360kW output, equivalent to about 483 horsepower, enables a 0–100km/h acceleration in just four seconds, making it a force to be reckoned with on the road.

Innovative Technology and Features

The C-Class's innovative technology features are equally impressive, showcasing Mercedes-Benz's commitment to pushing the boundaries of electric vehicle development. The two-speed transmission on the rear axle is a unique feature that enables seamless acceleration and maximum efficiency at highway speeds. Additionally, the front motor's disconnect unit allows for regenerative braking and improved energy recovery.

Anticipatory Suspension and AI-Powered Features

The C-Class's advanced air suspension system is equipped with anticipatory capabilities, using Google Maps data and Car-to-X communication to adjust damping milliseconds before impact. This intelligent feature is complemented by MB.Drive Assist Pro, which promises point-to-point assisted driving in approved locations.

Inside the cabin, the MBUX Hyperscreen dominates the dashboard, powered by the Unity game engine for enhanced graphics. A panoramic roof with 162 illuminated stars and a premium trim level featuring Burmester 4D Surround Sound System further enhance the luxurious atmosphere.

Infotainment and Augmented Reality

The C-Class's infotainment system features AI-powered capabilities, including a more conversational virtual assistant and native Google Maps integration. The augmented reality heads-up display projects 3D navigation cues and safety warnings directly into the driver's field of vision, providing an unparalleled driving experience.

Over-the-Air Updates and Future Outlook

The electric C-Class marks a significant step in Mercedes-Benz's electrification strategy, which promises a "flurry" of EV debuts this year. With its focus on plug-in power, the company has not abandoned its goal of transitioning to an all-electric lineup by 2030. As the automotive landscape continues to evolve, Mercedes-Benz is poised to remain at the forefront of innovation and luxury electric vehicle development.
