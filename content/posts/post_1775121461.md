The Artemis Moon base project has raised legal concerns, casting a shadow over NASA's ambitious plans to return humans to the lunar surface. As the space agency prepares to launch its Artemis II mission on Wednesday, with four astronauts aboard the Orion capsule, the scrutiny surrounding the project's legality is growing.

The current mission will see the crew travel around the Moon before returning to Earth in 10 days' time, testing out the hardware and systems that will form the foundation of the lunar base. However, questions have been raised about the legal framework underpinning the project. NASA has not publicly disclosed a comprehensive plan for managing the environmental, scientific, and cultural impacts of establishing a human settlement on the Moon.

Critics argue that the lack of transparency and clear regulations raises concerns about the long-term sustainability and potential consequences of the project. The international community is also grappling with how to address the legal implications of space exploration and development, as countries like China and private companies like SpaceX are increasingly active in space.

As NASA continues to push forward with its plans for a lunar base, it remains to be seen whether the agency will address these legal concerns and provide a clear framework for the project's future development.
