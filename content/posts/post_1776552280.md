In a significant development, AI chip startup Cerebras Systems has filed to go public, marking a major milestone in its rapid ascent to becoming one of the most valuable privately held companies in the industry. The company's decision to go public comes after a tumultuous year, which saw it raise a whopping $2.1 billion in funding and announce several high-profile partnerships.

Cerebras' impressive journey began when it started building what CEO Andrew Feldman describes as "the fastest AI hardware for training and inference." This unique value proposition has enabled the company to carve out a niche for itself in the highly competitive AI chip market, which is dominated by industry giants like Nvidia. In recent months, Cerebras has made significant strides in solidifying its position through strategic partnerships with Amazon Web Services (AWS) and OpenAI.

The partnership with AWS is particularly noteworthy, as it will see Cerebras chips being used in Amazon data centers. This move underscores the growing importance of edge AI, where real-time processing and inference are critical for applications like autonomous vehicles, smart cities, and more. The agreement with OpenAI, reportedly worth over $10 billion, is also a significant coup for Cerebras, as it has effectively taken away Nvidia's previously dominant position in this space.

Cerebras' financial performance has been equally impressive, with the company reporting revenue of $510 million in 2025 and a net income of $237.8 million (excluding certain one-time items). This is a remarkable achievement, especially considering that the company was still in the midst of recovering from its delayed IPO filing in 2024. The fact that Cerebras was able to bring in such significant revenue without going public suggests that it has been operating efficiently and effectively.

As Cerebras prepares for its mid-May IPO, investors are likely eager to learn more about its financial prospects. While the company has not disclosed how much it hopes to raise, the sheer scale of its funding round and partnerships makes it an attractive opportunity. The implications of Cerebras' success extend beyond the AI chip market itself, as it highlights the growing importance of edge AI and real-time processing in various industries. As the company continues to scale and expand, it will be fascinating to see how its IPO shapes the future of the industry.
