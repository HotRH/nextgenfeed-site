As the latest update to its Android offering, Google has given Chrome a sleek redesign that embodies the essence of Material 3 Expressive. The most notable change is the complete overhaul of the Settings page, which now features a modern and intuitive layout.

The new design replaces the previous text-based setup with neatly organized cards, separated by subtle padding at both ends. Each card boasts distinct corners, adding a touch of visual flair to the overall experience. Furthermore, the containers themselves are now lighter than the background, making for a clean and visually appealing contrast. This fresh approach not only enhances the glanceability of the settings but also aligns with the broader design trends in Android's Settings app.

One aspect that remains unchanged is the on/off toggle system, which continues to employ smaller switches compared to the rest of Android. However, when it comes to menu navigation, Chrome has adopted a similar card-based structure, providing users with an improved and more seamless experience.

Interestingly, this Material 3 Expressive update is being rolled out as a server-side update, requiring only a force stop of Chrome from the App info menu for those not yet seeing the revamp. This change joins a series of recent updates introduced last fall, including enhancements to the address bar, three-dot overflow menu, and Tab Grid.

In related news, Chrome has also seen improvements in other areas, such as the addition of a bookmarks bar on Android tablets and foldables, international expansion for Gemini, and a shift towards a two-week release cycle. As Chrome continues to evolve, users can expect even more exciting updates and features down the line.

"More on Chrome:

* You can now add the bookmarks bar to Chrome on Android tablets and foldables.
* Gemini in Chrome gets its first international expansion.
* Chrome is switching to a two-week release cycle."
