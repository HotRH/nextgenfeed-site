As you hold your new Samsung Galaxy S26, the first thought that pops into your mind is probably "what's the best case for me?" You've got three options - the S26, S26+, and S26 Ultra - but which one will keep your device safe from the rigors of daily use? In this article, we'll take you on a journey to explore the best cases and accessories available in the market.

When it comes to protecting your phone, a good case is more than just a fashion statement. It's about ensuring that your device remains intact, even when life gets unpredictable. Whether you're looking for rugged protection or sleek minimalism, we've got you covered with our top picks.

The Best Cases

Our top pick goes to the Spigen Tough Armor and Nano Pop MagFit cases. These affordable options offer great value for their price. While the Nano Pop case was a bit too slippery for our liking, the Tough Armor case has a sturdy kickstand that's perfect for watching videos or making hands-free calls.

Next up, we have the Dbrand Tank Case, which offers a tactical design that's sure to appeal to fans of rugged gear. With swappable buttons and camera covers, this case is a great option if you want to personalize your phone. However, the textured back can be overwhelming at times.

We also tested three cases from Poetic: the Spartan, Revolution, and Guardian. While they're not our top picks, these cases are still worth considering. The Revolution has a built-in camera privacy cover that's handy for protecting your personal space. However, the rest of the design is a bit bulkier than we'd like.

Magnetic Accessories

One thing to keep in mind when shopping for cases and accessories is the lack of native Qi2 support on the Samsung Galaxy S26 lineup. To take advantage of faster charging speeds and other benefits, you'll need a magnetic case. We've tested several options, but be aware that some magnetic wallets and power banks may not work well with the camera bump design.

How We Test Cases

At WIRED, we're committed to providing unbiased reviews and recommendations. When it comes to testing phone cases, we assess fit, comfort, overall look and feel, and button responsiveness. While we don't perform drop tests due to logistical constraints, we'll be working on standardizing a protocol in the future.

Cases to Avoid

Last but not least, there's one case that didn't make the cut: the Samsung Slim Magnet Case. At $70, this flimsy plastic shell with magnets is simply not worth the investment. There are many better options available for the same price or less.

Conclusion

Choosing the right case for your new Samsung Galaxy S26 is a crucial decision. With so many options on the market, it's easy to get overwhelmed. Our comprehensive guide has taken you through our top picks and recommendations, helping you make an informed decision that will keep your phone safe and sound. Whether you're looking for rugged protection or sleek minimalism, we've got you covered.
