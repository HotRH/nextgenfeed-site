The wait for Snap's augmented reality (AR) glasses, known as Specs, has been long and arduous. The company first began working on the project over a decade ago, with the last consumer-facing version released in 2019. Since then, the product has undergone significant changes, becoming a developer-only offering to allow Snap to refine its technology and build a foundation for future releases. Now, with the announcement of a partnership between Specs and chipmaker Qualcomm, it seems that the time is finally ripe for Snap's AR glasses to make their return.

The partnership, which was announced earlier this month, sees Specs leveraging Qualcomm's Snapdragon XR platforms to power its AR glasses. This move marks a significant turning point in the saga of Specs, as it brings the company's vision for the product one step closer to reality. By using Qualcomm's systems-on-a-chip (SoCs) designed specifically for augmented and virtual reality devices, Snap is able to tap into a wealth of expertise and resources that will help bring its AR glasses to market.

The agreement between Specs and Qualcomm goes beyond mere hardware, however. The two companies have committed to developing "on-device AI, cutting-edge graphics, and advanced multiuser digital experiences" as part of a multi-year strategic partnership. This collaboration has the potential to yield some truly innovative solutions, as both companies bring their unique strengths to the table. For Snap, this represents an opportunity to create a truly unique AR experience that will set its glasses apart from the competition.

Despite the setbacks and delays along the way, it's clear that Snap remains committed to bringing its AR glasses to market. In fact, CEO Evan Spiegel has spoken publicly about his excitement for the future of Specs, stating that the partnership with Qualcomm provides a "strong foundation" for the product. This renewed sense of optimism is palpable, and it will be interesting to see how the partnership between Specs and Qualcomm shapes up in the coming months.

The release of Snap's AR glasses is not just significant for the company itself; it also has implications for the broader AR industry. As a pioneer in the space, Snap's success (or failure) with its AR glasses could have a significant impact on the trajectory of AR adoption more broadly. With this new partnership in place, it will be exciting to see how Snap's AR glasses evolve and what innovative features they will bring to market. One thing is certain – after years of anticipation, the wait for Specs is finally over, and we can expect some truly exciting developments in the world of AR.
