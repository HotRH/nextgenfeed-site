In a move that sends shockwaves through the photo editing industry, Blackmagic Design has announced a significant update to its powerful post-production software, DaVinci Resolve 21. This latest version adds new tools and improved workflows for editing photos, positioning it as a formidable alternative to Adobe's Lightroom and Photoshop. But what does this mean for the future of photo editing?

A New Era in Photo Organization

At the heart of Resolve 21 is its new photo mode, designed to make it easier to organize, edit, and color correct multiple images. This feature is particularly noteworthy, as it allows users to apply AI-powered search functionality to find specific subjects or content within large collections of images. This level of organization and control was previously only available in specialized image management software.

A Shift Towards Flexibility

The new photo mode in Resolve 21 also includes a range of advanced color-correction and masking tools, which can be applied to still images with ease. Users can crop, reduce noise, sharpen, or apply "Film Looks" presets to streamline the editing process. The addition of support for RAW files from major camera manufacturers like Sony, Nikon, Canon, and Fujifilm further solidifies Resolve 21's position as a one-stop-shop for photographers.

The Cloud Connection

One of the most significant additions to Resolve 21 is its integration with Blackmagic Cloud, allowing for seamless multi-user collaboration. This feature enables teams to work together on projects in real-time, regardless of their location. Additionally, "GPU accelerated batch exports and conversions" enable workflows on tight deadlines to be sped up using graphics cards.

Tethered Cameras and Live Preview

Another innovative feature added to Resolve 21 is the ability to control tethered Sony and Canon cameras directly through the software's interface. This allows users to adjust settings like ISO, exposure, and white balance in real-time, while also providing a live preview of their shots. This level of integration with camera hardware has the potential to revolutionize the way photographers work.

The Future of Photo Editing

As DaVinci Resolve 21 becomes available as a free beta for macOS, Windows, and Linux, it is clear that this software is poised to shake up the photo editing industry. With its comprehensive feature set, seamless collaboration tools, and GPU acceleration, it will be interesting to see how Adobe's Lightroom and Photoshop respond to this new challenger. One thing is certain: the world of photo editing has just become a lot more exciting.
