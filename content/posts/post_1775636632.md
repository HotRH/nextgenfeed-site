In an era where smart TVs have plateaued in terms of innovation, TCL's QM9K series has managed to shake things up with its presence-sensing technology. This flagship-level Google TV set offers a unique experience that blurs the lines between passive and active viewing. With a 65-inch screen size, running on top of Google TV, this set is designed to immerse you in a cinematic experience like never before.

The QM9K's panel quality is nothing short of exceptional, boasting an impressive brightness of up to 6,500 nits in HDR. The vivid colors and dark blacks are sure to impress even the most discerning viewers. What's more, the viewing angles on this TV are incredibly wide, meaning that you can enjoy a great viewing experience from anywhere in the room.

In terms of hardware, the QM9K is also well-designed. Its sleek and slim profile makes it perfect for wall-mounting or placing on a table surface. The central base provides stability and the flat back allows for easy mounting of bias lighting strips.

The TV's port selection is also noteworthy, featuring four HDMI inputs, including one with ARC and another with full HDMI 2.1 bandwidth. You'll also find an Ethernet port, optical audio output, two USB ports, and a 3.5mm auxiliary port. While the lack of USB-C might be a drawback for some, it's a minor complaint in an otherwise well-equipped TV.

The remote control is intuitive and easy to use, with a premium feel thanks to its partially-metal finish. The layout is logical and straightforward, making it easy to navigate the TV's features.

But what really sets the QM9K apart is its Google TV experience. Fast and smooth, this platform offers all the benefits of a flagship-tier smart TV. TCL has preloaded some apps that are mostly useful, and the Settings menu is well-organized and easy to use.

However, the real pièce de résistance is the presence-sensing technology. This feature uses radar-like sensors to detect human presence in the room and automatically turn the TV on or off accordingly. The idea is to create an ambient experience where you can interact with the TV without having to physically touch it.

While this technology has its perks, I'm still a little unconvinced about its long-term usefulness. Having the TV automatically turn on and off based on who's in the room is nice, but the real benefit lies in being able to set the exact distance at which the TV reacts. This level of customization makes the feature feel more tailored to your viewing habits.

In conclusion, TCL's QM9K series is an impressive offering that brings a new level of innovation to the smart TV market. Its presence-sensing technology may not be a game-changer for everyone, but it's certainly a unique selling point that sets this TV apart from its competitors. With its excellent panel quality, thoughtful hardware design, and smooth Google TV experience, the QM9K is definitely worth considering if you're in the market for a new smart TV.
