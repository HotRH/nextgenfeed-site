In April 2025, the electric vehicle (EV) industry was shaken by the emergence of a new player - Slate Auto. What made this startup remarkable was its backing from none other than Jeff Bezos, one of the most influential figures in the tech world. But what really caught attention was Slate's bold plan to create an ultra-cheap, customizable EV pickup truck that would start at around $25,000.

A Secretive Start

For three years prior, Slate had been operating in stealth mode in Troy, Michigan - the backyard of major automakers like Ford and General Motors. This secrecy only added to the mystique surrounding the company, which was revealed by TechCrunch in April 2025. The report sparked a whirlwind of news as prototypes of the startup's truck began popping up around California.

A Differentiator in the EV Landscape

Slate Auto is an aberration in the US EV sector, where bankruptcies, failed product launches, and pivots have become commonplace. But Slate's approach seems different - it's focused on developing a deeply customizable electric pickup truck that leverages the experience of former employees from Harley-Davidson and Chrysler. This unique business model has the potential to disrupt the industry in a significant way.

Production Plans and Hurdles

As Slate pushes toward production in late 2026, several hurdles lie ahead. The company has identified a former printing plant in Warsaw, Indiana as the location for its truck factory. However, overcoming the challenges of setting up a new manufacturing facility will be crucial to the startup's success.

Reservations and Pricing

Slate's affordable pricing strategy seems to have resonated with consumers, with over 100,000 refundable reservations placed in just two weeks. The company had initially targeted an under-$20,000 price point for its truck, but this goal may need to be reevaluated 

A Bright Future Ahead

Despite these challenges, Slate Auto's innovative approach and backing from a tech giant like Jeff Bezos suggest that the company has a bright future ahead. As it navigates the complexities of setting up its manufacturing facility and addressing production hurdles, Slate is poised to make a significant impact in the EV landscape.
