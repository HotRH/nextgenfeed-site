As the world of professional cycling continues to push the boundaries of speed and performance, the need for innovative safety solutions has become increasingly pressing. In response, Van Rysel, a cutting-edge cycling apparel brand, has been working in collaboration with airbag technology specialist In&motion to develop a revolutionary wearable airbag system specifically designed for road cyclists. This game-changing innovation is currently being tested on professional riders ahead of its expected release to the general consumer market within the next two years.

The development of this wearable airbag system comes at a pivotal moment, as the Union Cycliste Internationale (UCI), the governing body of professional cycling, recently issued a call for gear that can help protect riders traveling at unprecedented speeds. The UCI's initiative has sparked a much-needed conversation about the importance of prioritizing rider safety in the sport.

The Van Rysel airbag system stands out from existing solutions due to its sleek and integrated design, which eliminates the need for bulky add-ons or attachments. Weighing approximately 700 grams (500 grams for the airbag components), this system is significantly lighter than those used in MotoGP, making it an attractive option for cyclists seeking protection without compromising performance.

The airbag deploys in just 60 milliseconds after its impact-detection algorithm detects a potential threat, providing rapid protection to the upper body not covered by a helmet. This includes critical areas such as the central core, cervical zone, and spinal line. While current testing focuses on protecting these regions, future developments aim to expand coverage to other parts of the body.

The Van Rysel product manager, Jocelyn Bar, emphasizes the human aspect of cycling safety: "Behind every race number, there's a human being... Sadly it is still widely accepted that a rider can lose everything in a fraction of a second due to a crash." The company's vision for Airbag is to provide a similar impact as helmets did two decades ago – revolutionizing protection and safeguarding the well-being of cyclists.

As the wearable airbag system inches closer to its release, it represents a significant step forward in the quest for enhanced rider safety. With its innovative design, rapid deployment, and comprehensive coverage, this technology has the potential to transform the cycling landscape and ensure that every ride is both exhilarating and safe.
