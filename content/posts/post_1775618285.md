In a move that signals significant progress in the world of mixed reality gaming, Valve has announced the release of its native Steam Link app on Apple's Vision Pro headset. This groundbreaking development allows Vision Pro users to play traditional games from their Steam library wirelessly from a nearby Mac or PC, marking a major milestone in the evolution of gaming technology.

What sets this apart is that it doesn't stream VR games – at least, not yet. The current iteration only supports traditional 2D games, which are typically played on computer monitors or TVs. This may seem like a limitation, but it's crucial to acknowledge that this is still a major breakthrough in its own right. After all, playing non-VR games wirelessly from a nearby computer using the Vision Pro headset offers a unique gaming experience that was previously only possible through third-party tools like ALVR.

The Steam Link app has been available on various Apple devices, including Macs, iPhones, iPads, and Apple TVs, allowing users to sync controllers and play Steam games over their local network. This new development takes the same concept and brings it to the Vision Pro, providing a more straightforward and reliable way to access a user's entire Steam library without the need for third-party software.

The current version of the app boasts impressive features, including support for 4K resolutions and dynamic display curve adjustments in panoramic mode. These capabilities demonstrate Valve's commitment to delivering a high-quality gaming experience on the Vision Pro. While the app is currently available via TestFlight, Apple's platform for distributing pre-release apps before they hit the App Store, it marks a significant step forward in making the Vision Pro a more attractive option for gamers.

In our review of the Vision Pro at launch, we highlighted its limitations in connecting to external gaming and media devices, as well as accessing mixed reality content outside Apple's ecosystem. Since then, Apple has made notable improvements, though there is still room for growth. The release of the Steam Link app on the Vision Pro represents a crucial step forward in addressing these issues and demonstrating the potential of this innovative technology. As the gaming landscape continues to evolve, it will be exciting to see how Valve and Apple continue to push the boundaries of what's possible with the Vision Pro and other mixed reality devices.
